# The BusyBits Weekly Review Checklist (Sunday Protocol)

**Objective:** Clear mental RAM, reset for the week, and align actions with goals.
**Time Required:** 30-45 Minutes.
**When:** Sunday Evening (Recommended) or Monday Morning (Early).

---

## Phase 1: Clear the Decks (Past)
*   [ ] **Inbox Zero:** Process all email inboxes to 0 (Archive, Reply, Defer/Task).
*   [ ] **Desktop Zero:** Clear desktop screenshots/files into appropriate folders or trash.
*   [ ] **Download Folder Zero:** Delete or file everything in Downloads.
*   [ ] **Physical Environment:** Clear desk surface. Reset workspace.
*   [ ] **Calendar Review (Last Week):** Look at the past 7 days.
    *   Did I complete all commitments?
    *   Any open loops/follow-ups needed? -> Add to Task Manager.

## Phase 2: Get Current (Present)
*   [ ] **Review Task List / Project List:**
    *   Mark completed items as done.
    *   Reschedule overdue items or delete them (be ruthless).
    *   Scan "Waiting For" list. Send follow-up pings.
*   [ ] **Review Metrics (KPIs):**
    *   Check Business KPIs (Revenue, Leads, Traffic).
    *   Check Health KPIs (Sleep Score, Workouts).
*   [ ] **Brain Dump:** Write down everything currently on your mind (open loops, ideas, worries). Get it out of your head and into the system.

## Phase 3: Plan the Attack (Future)
*   [ ] **Calendar Review (Next 2 Weeks):**
    *   What's coming up? (Deadlines, Meetings, Travel).
    *   Do I need to prepare anything *now* for these?
*   [ ] **Select "The Big 3":** What are the 3 non-negotiable wins for this week?
    1.  ______________________
    2.  ______________________
    3.  ______________________
*   [ ] **Time Blocking:** Block time on calendar for "The Big 3" (Deep Work blocks).
*   [ ] **Schedule "Life" Admin:** Workouts, Date Night, Family time.

---

## The "Sunday Scaries" Killer Questions
*   "What is one thing I can do tomorrow morning that will make the rest of the week easier?"
*   "Am I inventing work to avoid the hard work?"
*   "Who am I waiting on? Who is waiting on me?"

---
*Pro Tip: Do this with your favorite music/playlist to anchor the habit.*
