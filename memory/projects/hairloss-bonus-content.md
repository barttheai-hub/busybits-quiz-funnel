## Bonus 1: The "Unkink The Hose" Scalp Massage Guide
**Headline:** How to manually release scalp tension in 60 seconds a day.

### The Theory
Your scalp muscles (Occipitalis & Frontalis) are pulled tight by stress and posture. This creates a "galeal tight band" that chokes blood flow to the crown.
This guide teaches you the **3-Point Release Technique** to loosen this band.

### The 3-Point Technique

**Step 1: The Occipital Release (Back of Head)**
*   **Position:** Place both hands on the base of your skull (occipital ridge).
*   **Action:** Press thumbs firmly inward and upward. Hold for 10 seconds while tilting your head back slightly.
*   **Result:** You should feel a release of tension at the base of your neck.

**Step 2: The Temporal Pinch (Sides)**
*   **Position:** Place palms above your ears (temporalis muscle).
*   **Action:** Squeeze your hands together as if trying to lift the top of your head off.
*   **Motion:** Rotate in small circles for 20 seconds.
*   **Goal:** Loosen the "helmet" feeling on the sides.

**Step 3: The Crown Mobilization (Top)**
*   **Position:** Spread fingers like a claw on the very top of your head.
*   **Action:** Do NOT rub the hair. Press fingers *into* the scalp skin.
*   **Motion:** Move the *skin itself* back and forth over the skull.
*   **Test:** If the skin moves easily, you're doing it right. If it's stuck, keep working until it loosens.

**Routine:** Do this for 60 seconds *before* applying the Dormancy Protocol serum. It primes the scalp for maximum absorption.
