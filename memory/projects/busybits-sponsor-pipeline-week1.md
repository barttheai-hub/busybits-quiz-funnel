# BusyBits Sponsorship Pipeline — Week 1 Execution Pack

## Objective
Book 3–5 sponsor calls in 7 days from high-intent health/performance brands.

## ICP (ideal sponsor)
- DTC or subscription health/performance products
- Existing creator/newsletter spend
- Clear fit with BusyBits audience (productivity, sleep, health optimization)

## Priority verticals
1. Sleep optimization
2. Wearables / health tracking
3. Metabolic health
4. Supplements / nootropics
5. Men’s health

## Outreach sequence (copy blocks)
### Email 1 — Pattern interrupt
Subject: Quick BusyBits sponsor idea

"Hey {{firstName}} —

I run BusyBits (95k+ subscribers, health/performance focused).
I think {{brand}} is a strong fit for our audience based on {{specific angle}}.

If useful, I can send 2 package options and expected range for clicks/signups based on recent sends.

Open to that?"

### Email 2 — value follow-up (48h)
Subject: Re: BusyBits sponsor idea

"Can send over:
- 2 sponsorship formats (native + dedicated)
- expected CTR range by placement
- recommended test budget + scale path

Worth sharing?"

### Email 3 — close loop (72h)
Subject: Close this out?

"No pressure — should I close this out for now, or circle back next month?"

## Fast qualification checklist (before sending)
- Brand has active paid social
- Brand has newsletter/influencer history
- Landing page and offer are conversion-ready
- One concrete angle tying brand -> BusyBits audience

## Daily execution cadence
- 15 new outbound messages/day
- 10 follow-ups/day
- 1 pipeline review/day (wins, objections, blocked leads)

## KPI targets (Week 1)
- 75 first touches
- 35%+ reply rate target on warm-fit list
- 10+ positive replies
- 3–5 booked calls

## Objection handling snippets
- "No budget": "Happy to run a small test placement first and use performance data to size next steps."
- "Not now": "Got it — I can follow up in 4 weeks with one audience-fit idea and recent performance benchmarks."
- "Need proof": "Can share anonymized recent performance ranges and recommended creative format for your category."
